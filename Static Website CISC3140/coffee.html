Doppio

About:

In Italian doppio means ‘double’. So a doppio espresso simple means two shots of same volume espresso. End up you will have double volume of normal single shot of espresso. Doppio also extracted through double layer of coffee filter.

Ingredients:

- Freshly roasted dark espresso coffee beans

Methods:

- Grind the fresh coffee bean into powder.
- Place espresso bean powder into the portafilter or group of the espresso machine, tamper it hard.
- The group should be double layer.
- Turn on espresso machine.
- Wait for 18-25 seconds and you will have your espresso.

Americano

About:

What is a café Americano? It is made by adding hot water to an espresso shot. The water dilutes the espresso a bit, giving you the volume of a drip, but with an espresso taste. Read on to learn how to make the perfect tasting Americano yourself!

Ingredients:

- 14–18 grams espresso coffee beans
- hot or almost boiling water
- espresso machine
- grinder and scale

Methods:

- Measure out your beans and grind them.
- Make the espresso.
- Heat up the water to 160-170 degrees Fahrenheit.
- Pour the espresso into the hot water, mix 1 part espresso to 2 parts water.

Macchiato

About:

Macchiato in Italian means “stained”, so a caffé macchiato means “stained coffee”. It is espresso with a small amount of milk added. A Caffè Macchiato is a small simple drink of an espresso shot or double shot marked with a touch of delicious frothy milk.

Ingredients:

- 1 to 2 shots of espresso
- A dollop of foamed milk (with the texture of melted ice cream)

Methods:

- In a macchiato cup top the espresso with a dollop of steamed milk.
- Serve immediately.

Flat White

About:

A flat white has one or two shots of espresso (just like lattes and flat whites) but microfoamed milk is used to create a velvety texture with no foam topper – the milk runs right through the coffee.

Ingredients:

- 1 shot espresso
- 2 shots foamed milk

Methods:

- Pull 1 shot espresso.
- While pulling the shot, froth the milk.
- Use a spoon to fold the microbubbles.
- Combine the two ingredients.
- Your flat white should have about 1/4-inch of steamed milk on top.

Cappuccino

About:

In Italian, cappuccino means "little cap," which perfectly describes the head of foamed milk that sits atop the drink's espresso base. According to history, it also allegedly derives from the dress of the Capuchin order of monks. With their iconic brown hooded cowls and shaved heads, Capuchin monks are a pretty close human resemblance to the ring of crema and white foam that tops the classic beverage.

Ingredients:

- 2 shots
- 4 ounces milk

Methods:

- Pull a double shot of espresso into a cappuccino cup.
- Foam the milk to double its original volume.
- Top the espresso with foamed milk right after foaming.
- Serve immediately.

Caffe Latte

About:

Caffe Lattes are espresso drinks made with a fairly large amount of foamed milk. They are milkier than cappuccinos. In America, many lattes are prepared as flavored lattes. In France, caffè latte is mostly known from the original Italian name of the drink.

Ingredients:

- 1 shot espresso
- 2 shots foamed milk

Methods:

- Pull your espresso shot in your latte cup.
- Using a spoon to retain the microbubbles on top of the steamed milk.
- Pour the bottom 2/3 of the steamed milk from the steaming pitcher into the latte cup.
- Top the latte with the remaining bubbles.
- Serve and enjoy!
